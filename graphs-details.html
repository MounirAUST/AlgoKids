Graphs

1. What is a Graph?

A graph is a structure that represents relationships between objects. Objects are called nodes (or vertices), and the connections are called edges.

Graphs can be directed or undirected, weighted or unweighted.

2. Why Graphs Are Useful

Graphs help us model and solve problems such as:

  • Social networks
  • Maps and navigation
  • Web page links
  • Electrical circuits

They are used everywhere from internet routing to AI pathfinding!

3. Key Concepts

  • Vertex: A point/node in the graph
  • Edge: A connection between two vertices
  • Adjacency List: A way to store edges using lists
  • Adjacency Matrix: A grid to represent connections
