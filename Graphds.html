The ACTIONS panel on the bottom left displays all available operations on this data structure. Experiment with them!

The STATUS panel on the right displays the status of the current operation. The CODETRACE panel below it displays the pseudocode for the current operation, and uses code highlighting to show you which step is being carried out.

During each action, you can use the playback controls at the bottom of the screen to control the animation. Note that you have to click 'stop' first before you can issue another action.

About the Project

What is AlgoKids?
AlgoKids is an interactive educational platform developed by senior students at the American University of Science and Technology (AUST). It's designed to help children learn the fundamentals of Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) in a fun and engaging way.

The idea behind AlgoKids is simple — we believe computer science doesn’t have to be difficult or boring, especially for kids. So we created something visual, playful, and easy to understand, using animations and intuitive controls.

Why We Built It
Most DSA tools are made for college students. We wanted to make one for kids — something that breaks down complex concepts into visuals they can follow and learn from. Whether it’s a linked list or a queue, AlgoKids shows how it works step by step.

What’s Next?
We’re working on adding new features like quizzes, challenges, and more data structures. Our goal is to make AlgoKids a go-to resource for young learners stepping into the world of programming and algorithms.

The Team

This project was developed as part of a senior capstone at the American University of Science and Technology (AUST). The team worked together to build AlgoKids with the goal of simplifying DSA education for kids.
